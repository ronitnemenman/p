Golden Box

Piece Information

  • Dimensions (in):
  • Materials:
    • Charcoal
    • KingArt Water Soluble Crayons
    • Prisma Color Pencil
  • Estimated Pricing: --
  • Month Produced:10/24

Golden Box was the visual representation of my feelings after my grandfathers death. I pushed myself into escapism until I hit a breaking point, until I couldn't stop remembering. The same way the poem itself is in black and white and while the box is almost entirely insonspicous its gold coloring draws your eye. You know its important no matter how plain in shape and style it is.

The following is a collection of process and progress photos taken during the creation of this piece along with explanations

A timelapse of my beginning on working in charcoal, showing a lot of the small adjustments I had to make to spacing and the piece and the still life itself

After working in charcoal for about a week, the piece was ready to seal and co over in water soluble crayon to create a subtle but unmistakable gold.

Using a ruler I wrote as much of the poem as I could to create a blurred but meangful background for the piece.
