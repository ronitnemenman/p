Warped

Piece Information

  • Dimensions (in): 31 x 16.5
  • Materials:
    • Wood
    • Caran d'Ache Water Soluble Crayon
    • Dotting Paint Marker
    • Drawing Gum
    • Water Soluble Color Pencil
    • Various Other Water Soluble Crayons
  • Estimated Pricing: 125$
  • Month Produced: 10/24

This piece represents the most literal definition of escapism. Looking at reality and choosing to ignore it in favor of something more preferential.

The following is a collection of process and progress photos taken during the creation of this piece along with explanations

Initially, I tested 4 different methods to keep the tentacles clear of the dark green on the wood so that the tentacles would seem to really glow.

Process photo of me peeling off the resists during the next step of testing.

The test would ended up deciding that masking fluid would win withot a doubt. It prevented leaking through the grain as well as giving me control of the shape that the tapes did not, and it did not fracture the wood like the glues.

A progress photo of the drawing gum in place on the wood before I began use of the water soluble crayon

I then cut out the scene from the wood with a jigsaw, choosing the shape of my substrate.

Process photo of the peeling of the drawing gum to reveal the would before layering.

Close up of the various textures such as the ice, the grass, the tentacles, and their glow.
